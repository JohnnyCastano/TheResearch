  1. Motorola's DynaTAC 8000X
    , the name of the first ever mobile cellphone, one of the greatest milestones in the history of technology. This technological masterpiece was created by a Motorola engineer named Martin Cooper. The first ever mobile telephone was, as some say, "the brick", and they were not wrong. The telephone weighed about two pounds and was not very practical, although this changed the world. The telephone was almost three times the size of the first ever iPhone. The telephone was rectangular shaped, it had and antenna at the top, it then had a speaker at the top of the telephone case. At the bottom of it there was a microphone. In between the speaker and the microphone was buttons with number and buttons that said end, lock, etc... below the numbers. Inside of the telephone was a synthesizer(it generated 380 channels electronically), a battery (the battery delivers 14-volts of power for 36 minutes of transmission), a reciever, a supervisory unit (it is the brain, it recieves and sends digital signals to central computers), a triselector (a series of transmission lines), and a transmitter. The circuits are separated with soldered sheet metal. The phones battery took ten hours to charge, that only lasted about 30 minutes, and could only save a total of 30 phone numbers. When it officially was released to the public, the price of the telephone was $3,995 dollars. Today the DynaTac 8000x, or "the brick" goes for around $10,000, and is a milestone in technology that will never be forgotten.

It wasn't until many years later that the first ever text message was actually sent. The first message was sent on December 3rd, 1992 by Neil Papworth (former developer at Sema Group Telecoms), Neil at that time sent the message on a pc since telephones did not have keyboards at that time, the message was sent to Richard Jarvis at Vodafane, the message said "Merry Christmas", it was successfully sent. Texting did not work with telephones until the Nokia 9000i came out in 1997 (with a full keyboard) which supported the use of SMS text messages. Although it was not much of use at the beginning since it could not send messsages to different people over long distances. It wasn't until several years later, in the year 2000 when texting could be done over different networks, which increased its usefulness. Now (in that time) the average American sent around 35 texts a month. Now texting can be done in multiple different ways, through multiple different platforms like messenger, snapchat, instagram and more all over the world. Texting has revolutionized the way human beings communicate. Texting can now be done in milliseconds from anywhere in the world. Its one of the greates milestones in technology.

  4. The Internet
    The internet, some say it is the most important invention in the 21st century, and the most important in technological advancements. The idea of the internet was brought by one of the greatest inventors of his time, Nikola Tesla. Tesla thought of the idea of a "world wireless system". It was not until several years later that the internet was created. In the early 1960's, MIT J.C.R. Licklider introduced the idea of an "Intergalactic Network" of computers. Not long after several computer scientists developed the concept of "packet switching", a method that allowed for transmitting electronic data, this was a building block for the internet. The first workable prototype of the internet cam in the late 1960's, with the creation of ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network). It used packet switching to allow several computers to work on a singular network. On October 29, 1969, the ARPAnet delivered its first message, it was a "node-to-node" communication from one computer to another. One computer was in UCLA, while the other in Stanford. The message said "LOGIN", but it immediately crashed the ARPA network. The Stanford computer only recieved the messages first two letters. Technology only continued to grow from there. On January 1, 1983, ARPANET adopted TCP/IP, researchers began to assemble the "network of networks", this became the moder internet. This is as well one of technologies greatest milestones that changed our lives forever, all the information in the world in our hands.
